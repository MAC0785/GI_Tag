Textile

Mysore Silk

Origin: Mysore, Karnataka

Description: Mysore Silk is one of India's most luxurious silks, produced by the Karnataka Silk Industries Corporation. It is made from pure mulberry silk and is known for its lustrous texture, rich colors, and durability.

Characteristics: Soft texture, natural sheen, rich zari work, and traditional motifs. The silk is tested for quality at every stage of production.

Famous Products: Mysore silk sarees are highly sought after for weddings and special occasions.

GI Tag Year: 2005

Source: Karnataka Silk Industries Corporation / GI Registry

Agricultural

Coorg Coffee

Origin: Kodagu (Coorg) District, Karnataka

Description: Coorg Coffee, both Arabica and Robusta varieties, is grown in the misty hills of Kodagu. The region's elevation, rainfall pattern, and soil create ideal conditions for coffee cultivation.

Flavor Profile: Arabica has mild, aromatic flavor with chocolate undertones. Robusta is bold with earthy notes and higher caffeine content.

Cultivation: Grown under shade trees in a traditional multi-crop system, making it environmentally sustainable.

GI Tag Year: 2011

Handicraft

Channapatna Toys

Origin: Channapatna, Ramanagara District, Karnataka

Description: Channapatna toys are traditional wooden toys and dolls, colored with natural dyes. This 200-year-old craft is known for its smooth finish and vibrant colors.

Unique Features: Made from ivory wood (Wrightia tinctoria), turned on lathes, and painted with lac (natural resin). Completely safe for children as no toxic colors are used.

Recognition: Often called "Gombegala Ooru" (Toy-town) of Karnataka.

GI Tag Year: 2006

Source: Karnataka Handicrafts Development Corporation / GI Registry

Handicraft

Mysore Agarbathi (Incense Sticks)

Origin: Mysore, Karnataka

Description: Mysore Agarbathi is renowned for its unique fragrance derived from natural ingredients including sandalwood oil, which is abundant in Karnataka.

Ingredients: Made from natural resins, essential oils (especially sandalwood), herbs, and aromatic powders rolled on bamboo sticks.

Special Features: Long-lasting fragrance, slow burning, and therapeutic properties. Used in temples, meditation, and aromatherapy.

GI Tag Year: 2005

Handicraft

Mysore Sandalwood Oil

Origin: Mysore region, Karnataka

Description: Mysore Sandalwood (Santalum album) oil is considered the finest sandalwood oil in the world, extracted from the heartwood of sandalwood trees aged 30-40 years.

Properties: Contains high levels of alpha and beta santalol (essential aromatic compounds), making it superior in fragrance and therapeutic value.

Uses: Perfumery, cosmetics, pharmaceuticals, aromatherapy, and religious ceremonies.

GI Tag Year: 2005

Textile

Ilkal Sarees

Origin: Ilkal, Bagalkot District, Karnataka

Description: Ilkal sarees are traditional handloom sarees known for their distinctive joining technique of cotton warp and silk weft called "Tope Teni."

Design Elements: Characteristic red border with white patterns, temple designs, and geometric patterns. The pallu (end piece) has a unique kasuti embroidery-inspired design.

GI Tag Year: 2006

Agricultural

Nanjangud Banana

Origin: Nanjangud, Mysore District, Karnataka

Description: Nanjangud Banana is a unique variety known for its distinctive taste, aroma, and creamy texture. Grown along the banks of river Kapila.

Characteristics: Medium-sized fruit, golden yellow when ripe, sweet with a subtle tangy note, and rich in natural sugars and potassium.

Cultural Significance: Offered as prasadam (offering) in the famous Srikanteshwara Temple.

GI Tag Year: 2006

Handicraft

Bidriware

Origin: Bidar, Karnataka

Description: Bidriware is a metal handicraft from Bidar featuring silver inlay work on a blackened alloy of zinc and copper. This 600-year-old Persian-influenced craft is unique to India.

Process: Intricate designs are carved into the metal, filled with pure silver wire, and then the surface is darkened using a special soil treatment unique to Bidar.

Products: Vases, bowls, trays, hookah bases, jewelry boxes with floral and geometric patterns.

GI Tag Year: 2006
