Textile

Chanderi Fabric

Origin: Chanderi, Ashoknagar District, Madhya Pradesh

Description: Chanderi fabric is a traditional handloom fabric known for its sheer texture, lightweight, and glossy transparency. It combines cotton and silk threads to create elegant designs.

Characteristics: Distinctive gold and silver zari work, traditional motifs like coins, flowers, and peacocks.

GI Tag Year: 2008

Textile

Maheshwar Sarees

Origin: Maheshwar, Khargone District, Madhya Pradesh

Description: Maheshwar sarees are handwoven cotton and silk sarees with distinctive borders and pallus. They feature reversible borders and are known for their elegant simplicity.

Design: Stripes, checks, and floral patterns with gold zari borders.

GI Tag Year: 2008

Handicraft

Gond Paintings

Origin: Central India (Madhya Pradesh)

Description: Gond paintings are a form of folk art practiced by the Gond tribal community. They use natural colors and depict nature, folklore, and tribal life.

Style: Bright colors, intricate patterns, dots and lines creating detailed imagery.

GI Tag Year: 2010

Textile

Bagh Print

Origin: Bagh, Dhar District, Madhya Pradesh

Description: Bagh print is a traditional hand block printing technique using natural dyes. Known for its geometric and floral patterns in earthy colors.

Process: Uses natural colors like indigo, turmeric, and pomegranate rind.

GI Tag Year: 2010
