Agricultural

Assam Tea

Origin: Assam Valley, Assam

Description: Assam tea is known worldwide for its robust, malty flavor, bright color, and strong, brisk character. Grown in the plains of the Brahmaputra valley, it's the largest tea-growing region in the world.

Characteristics: Full-bodied, rich aroma with malty notes, ideal for breakfast blends. Contains high levels of antioxidants.

History: Commercial cultivation began in 1839 with the discovery of indigenous tea plants.

GI Tag Year: 2005

Source: Tea Board of India / GI Registry

Textile

Muga Silk

Origin: Assam

Description: Muga silk is the rarest and most expensive silk in the world, produced only in Assam. Known as the "golden silk," it has a natural golden luster and exceptional durability.

Unique Features: Made from semi-cultivated silkworm Antheraea assamensis, found exclusively in Assam. Becomes shinier with age and washing.

Cultural Significance: Traditional attire of Assamese royalty and aristocracy.

GI Tag Year: 2007

Agricultural

Joha Rice

Origin: Assam

Description: Joha is an aromatic scented rice variety native to Assam, with a distinctive fragrance similar to jasmine. It's grown using traditional methods without chemicals.

Characteristics: Short-grain sticky rice, aromatic fragrance, soft texture when cooked. Rich in antioxidants and has low glycemic index.

Varieties: Kola Joha, Boga Joha, Keteki Joha each with unique properties.

GI Tag Year: 2018

Agricultural

Assam Karbi Anglong Ginger

Origin: Karbi Anglong District, Assam

Description: This ginger variety is known for its high oil content, strong aroma, and medicinal properties. The hilly terrain and climate of Karbi Anglong provide ideal growing conditions.

Characteristics: High fiber content, pungent taste, rich in essential oils (2-3%), golden yellow color.

Uses: Culinary spice, traditional medicine, essential oil extraction.

GI Tag Year: 2013

Handicraft

Assam Gamusa

Origin: Assam

Description: Gamusa is a traditional handwoven cotton towel with red border and traditional motifs. It holds immense cultural significance in Assamese society.

Cultural Importance: Used in religious ceremonies, as a sign of respect, and presented to guests. Symbol of Assamese identity and hospitality.

Design: White or off-white body with red border and traditional patterns at both ends.

GI Tag Year: 2022
