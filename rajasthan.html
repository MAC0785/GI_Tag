Handicraft

Blue Pottery of Jaipur

Origin: Jaipur, Rajasthan

Description: Blue Pottery is a traditional craft from Jaipur, characterized by its distinctive blue dye and unique glazing technique. Unlike regular pottery, it uses no clay but a mixture of quartz stone powder, powdered glass, and Multani Mitti.

Unique Features: Turquoise blue color, floral motifs, geometric patterns. The pottery is completely non-porous and eco-friendly.

GI Tag Year: 2015

Textile

Bandhani

Origin: Rajasthan

Description: Bandhani is a tie-dye textile art form where fabric is tied in small points and dyed to create intricate patterns. This ancient technique produces vibrant designs with spiritual significance.

Technique: Fabric is tied with thread at multiple points, then dyed. The tied portions resist color, creating unique patterns.

GI Tag Year: 2010

Mining

Makrana Marble

Origin: Makrana, Nagaur District, Rajasthan

Description: Makrana Marble is world-famous white marble used in constructing the Taj Mahal. Known for its pure white color and fine texture.

Famous Use: Taj Mahal, Victoria Memorial, and various Rajasthani palaces.

GI Tag Year: 2006

Handicraft

Bagru Print

Origin: Bagru, Jaipur District, Rajasthan

Description: Bagru Print is a traditional hand block printing technique using natural dyes and wooden blocks. Known for earthy colors and geometric patterns.

Process: Cotton fabric is block-printed using natural indigo, madder, and other vegetable dyes.

GI Tag Year: 2010
